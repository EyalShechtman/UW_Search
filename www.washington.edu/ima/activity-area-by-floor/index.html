Activity Areas and Offerings by Floor

4th Floor

Cardiovascular Training

Adaptive Motion Trainers


Adaptive Motion Trainers: The AMT offers multiple workout options – walking, running, stepping, even climbing – by automatically adapting to your movement. It has over 7,200 movement combinations.

Cross Trainers

Cross Trainers: One machine does it all, offering a combined cardio and strength workout. The machine appeals to all exercisers, from those looking for intense power training to those who want a low-impact cross-training session.

Ellipticals

Ellipticals: A premium elliptical cross-trainer that combines expert biomechanics and a natural-feeling motion. The result is an inviting, low-impact cardio option for a wide range of exercisers.

Indoor Rowers


Indoor Rowers: The Concept 2 Rower provides a more comprehensive, full-body workout of any fitness machine. It’s low-impact enough for use in physical rehabilitation centers and intensive enough for the world’s fittest athletes.

Indoor Cycling Bikes


Indoor Cycling Bikes: Designed with all the ergonomic features and capabilities of a real road bike. Flywheel simulates the movements and feel of real cycling and minimizes the stress on your knees.

Recumbent Bikes

Recumbent Bikes: For who prefer a seated position and enhanced engagement. The easy step-through design makes it suitable for all exercisers.

Stair Climbers

Stair Climbers: The step design includes added cushion and independent pedal geometry, with four-bar linkage to create a fluid, natural stepping motion.

Stepmills


Stepmills: Delivers a heart-pounding workout with a great calorie burn through a low-impact stair climb.

Treadmills


Treadmills: Features speeds up to 12 MPH and a 15 % incline elevation range for uphill training.

Upright Bikes


Upright Bikes: Comfort is provided by an adjustable padded seat and wide ride pedals. Deluxe racing handlebars let exercisers choose positioning for relaxed rides or more intense pedaling.

Indoor Running Track

Indoor Running Track


Indoor Running Track features include:

  • Climate control: there is no rain, snow, heat, or wind to contend with on an indoor track.
  • Closed course: you can time yourself and find your speed because the distance around the track is known and posted.
  • Direction changes daily: This keeps the running experience varied and reduces wear on one side of the track.
  • Nine Laps = One Mile: This provides a clear measurement for tracking distance.
  • Two Running Lanes: Dedicated lanes for runners ensure a smooth experience.
  • Two Walking Lanes: Separate lanes for walkers to accommodate different types of users and activities.

Strength Training

Dumbbells

Dumbbells: Customize your range of motion, challenge stability, work different planes of motion, with these form of functional, beginner-friendly free weights.

Multi-Adjustable Benches

Multi-Adjustable Benches: multiple seat and back positions to work all muscle groups with an extended range of motion.

Pulley Machines


Pulley Machines: Multi-faceted pieces of equipment that can be customized with various add-ons, configurations and setups to provide exercisers with nearly limitless workout variety. Employs user-defined paths of motion that allow for a nearly endless variety of strength training options that builds balance, stability, and power.

Selectorized Machines


Selectorized Machines: High quality weight stacks, guide rods, pulleys and cables create a smooth feel based on natural biomechanics.

Straight Barbells


Straight Barbells: This free weight option come pre-loaded with set weights allowing you to utilize your own stabilizing muscles and coordination rather than using a machine.

Stretching/Core Exercise

Balance Trainers


Balance Trainers: Users of any skill level and from any discipline can benefit from using the balance trainer for a wide range of movements; from core and cardio work to basic balance and mobility drills.

Foam Rollers


Foam Rollers: Used during pre-workout, recovery, or just in spare time during the day, this type of deep tissue therapy, or self-myofascial release, can improve flexibility and balance, help eliminate long term muscle pain and adhesions, and reduce the risk of more serious injuries over time.

Gym Mats


Gym Mats: provide a safe, supportive, and comfortable surface for exercises.

Stability Balls


Stability Balls: Promote proper posture, increase lower back mobility, build back and abdominal strength.

Stretch Cage


Stretch Cage: The stretch cage provides a dedicated, self-contained stretching area that allows users a safe platform for comprehensive stretching to increase flexibility.

Wall Mounted Ballet Bars


Wall Mounted Ballet Bars: use these devices to gradually warm up, train muscles for turnout and proper alignment, and build stability on the standing leg.

Sport Courts

Two Basketball Courts


Two Basketball Courts: a game played between two teams of five players in which goals are scored by throwing a ball through a netted hoop fixed above each end of the court. Basketballs are available at the Equipment Issue Area on the first floor.

Two Volleyball Courts

Two Volleyball Courts: a game for two teams, usually of six players, in which a large ball is hit by hand over a high net, the aim being to score points by making the ball reach the ground on the opponents side of the court. Volleyballs are available at the Equipment Issue Area on the first floor.

Hangboard


Hangboard: Improve finger, grip, back, and core strength while training a variety of grip positions and isolation exercises. Enhance climbing performance, power endurance, and maximum strength.

MoonBoard


MoonBoard: A standardized interactive training wall that connects a global community of climbers through shared problems and competitive performance rankings. The app allows users to browse, filter, select, and illuminate problems on the MoonBoard. Each user’s logbook provides a detailed overview of completed ascents, problem ratings, and global ranking.

Equipment Issue Area

General Information

Most items of sports equipment related to IMA may be checked out with a valid Husky ID Card, Rec membership card, or guest receipt. Sports equipment game bags are available and may be checked out with a valid Husky ID Card or Rec membership card. Softball bag includes gloves, bat, balls, and a catcher’s mask. Volleyball bag includes net, volleyball, and various poles, stakes, and ropes. Tug-o-war bag includes rope. Learn more about the types of equipment available for checkout here. Fine policy – participants are financially responsible for any item of equipment checked out for their use or issued to their guests. See the Equipment Issue area for additional details.

Mat Rooms

Description


Two fully matted (floor and walls) rooms: Provides a safe, supportive, and comfortable surface for exercises. Used for combative sports and martial arts.

Sport Courts

Squash Courts


Two International Squash Courts: A racket and ball sport played by two or four players in a four-walled court with a small, hollow, rubber ball. The players alternate striking the ball with their rackets onto the playable surfaces of the four walls of the court. Equipment is available at the Equipment Issue Area on the first floor.

Racquetball Courts


Four Racquetball Courts: A game played with a small hard ball and a short-handled racket in a four-walled handball court. Equipment is available at the Equipment Issue Area on the first floor.

Step Hurdles: Improve knee lift and step quickness. Position hurdles for agility drills, running drills, and plyometric jumps. Use with forward and lateral drills.
