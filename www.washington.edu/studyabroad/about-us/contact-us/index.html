Best practices when contacting us

We are here to help you. To ensure the best assistance, please follow these guidelines.

  • Search our website first. Check out the Quick Links & FAQ.
  • Choose the appropriate contact method from the options below. Email is the fastest way to reach us for all types of questions.
  • Contacting us with the same question or request through multiple channels may result in a delayed response.

Advising Types

  • General Advising
    • For current UW students, prospective UW students, or Non-UW students seeking study abroad opportunities
    • General advising is for you if you:
      • don’t know where to start
      • are not sure which program is right for you
      • have individual circumstances and challenges
    • General advising does not replace program-specific advising. Scheduling the right type of advising helps you save time
  • Scholarships and Funding Advising
    • Scholarships and funding advising can help you navigate through different funding options including financial aid and scholarships
  • Program-Specific Advising
    • Program-specific advising is for you if you:
      • are interested in
      • or are applying/have applied to an Exchange, Partner, Direct Enrollment, or FRILA program
  • Credits and Grades Advising
    • Credits and grades advising is for you if you have read this page and this page and still have questions about credits and grades
    • Credits and grades advising DOES NOT provide UW equivalent credits for foreign courses. For credit equivalencies, please contact departmental/academic advisers with course syllabi.
    • Study abroad advisers will not advise you on how your study abroad credits fit into your major or degree requirements. Please reach out to your academic adviser.
    • Credits and grades advising are NOT for students on a Faculty-led Program

  • For expert guidance, meet with a study abroad adviser.
  • Select the appropriate type of advising to prevent the need for additional appointments.
  • Advising is NOT intended for quick questions.
  • All appointments are one-on-one via Zoom.

Inquiries for your family member

While we encourage students to take responsibility for their study abroad experience, parents and family members are welcome to email us at studyabroad@uw.edu for general questions about our UW Study Abroad programs. Please note that we can only provide general information that can be found on our website.

Privacy considerations

UW Study Abroad must abide by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). This can mean that we cannot divulge student academic, financial or enrollment information to parents without the student’s written consent. If you are making an inquiry on behalf of your student regarding their academic, financial, or enrollment information, please include written consent from your student in your email message and copy your student on that email. Find out more information about student educational privacy rights.
